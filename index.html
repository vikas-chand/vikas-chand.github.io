About Me

"Per aspera ad astra"

Astrophysicist & Researcher

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Louisiana State University (LSU), specializing in high-energy transients including gamma-ray bursts and magnetar giant flares. My research emphasizes Fermi-LAT data analysis, multi-wavelength observations, and physics-driven modeling of extreme astrophysical phenomena. Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Astrophysics Research Center of the Open University (ARCO) in Israel.

Research Focus

High-Energy Astrophysics & Transients

My research focuses on understanding the most energetic phenomena in the universe through multi-wavelength observations and theoretical modeling.

  • Gamma-ray bursts (GRBs) - prompt and afterglow emission
  • Magnetar giant flares and their connection to GRBs
  • High-energy polarimetry with AstroSat-CZTI
  • Fermi-LAT data analysis and transient detection
  • Very-high-energy (TeV) emission mechanisms
  • Time-resolved spectral analysis
